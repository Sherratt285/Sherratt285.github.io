Emotional Intelligence and Intelligence Quotient


Written by: Sherratt Pemberton
published: 30/09/2021

Introduction

At our core humans are social creatures - our society is built around teamwork after all. Teamwork is what allowed us to become the dominant species on this planet. Many hands make light work after all, and cover each-others different strengths and weaknesses.

Fortunately, the skills that allow us to work effectively in teams can be learnt – they are critical to our survival after all. This set of skills is referred to as EI or Emotional Intelligence.

EI or Emotional intelligence is our ability to understand, express and control our emotions, and understand the emotions of others. It helps us resolve conflicts, and effectively work with other people.
EI is normally built around for parts.

self Management - or our ability to control impulsive feelings and manage our emotions in a constructive way and adapt to changing circumstances.

Self Awareness – our awareness of our own emotions and how they effect our behaviour.

Social Awareness – our understanding of the emotions and concerns of other people – or empathy.

Relationship Management – how we develop and maintain relationships, resolve conflicts, and work together as a team.

IQ or Intelligence Quotient. The idea is to measure someone’s problem solving and reasoning ability to provide a guidance on which kind of job they might be suited for. This is found using a sequence of tests based on short term and long term memory, under a time limit which creates a stress factor. And represented as a score.
While it can be an interesting insight into someone’s strengths and weaknesses, it is no indicator for success. As there are many other personality, and environmental factors at play.

Conclusion

In summery. Emotional intelligence is our ability to be aware of and control (to an extent) our emotions, and the emotions of others and is definitely something we can learn. IQ is a measure of our ability to solve problems using logic and reasoning. Both are useful measurements that can help to gauge someone’s suitability for a particular position in society. But. It is important to remember they are only guidelines, and as the saying goes “it’s not over until the fat lady sings.”
